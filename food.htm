Most popular food in Egypt

Koshari

koshari, is a popular Egyptian street food and national dish. It is a hearty and flavorful mix of rice, lentils, and pasta, topped with a spiced tomato sauce and garnished with crispy fried onions. Often, chickpeas and garlic vinegar are added, and it is typically served with a side of hot sauce. Koshary is beloved for its comforting and filling nature, offering a delicious blend of textures and flavors.

molokhia

Molokhia, also known as Mulukhiyah or Jute Mallow, is a traditional Middle Eastern and North African dish made from the leaves of the Corchorus plant. The leaves are finely chopped and cooked into a thick, green soup or stew, often flavored with garlic, coriander, and sometimes lemon. It is typically served over rice or with flatbread and accompanied by meats such as chicken or rabbit. Known for its rich, slightly slimy texture and savory taste, Molokhia is a beloved comfort food in many countries, including Egypt, Lebanon, and Palestine.

falafel

Ta'meya, also known as falafel in other parts of the world, is a popular Middle Eastern dish made from ground chickpeas or fava beans mixed with herbs and spices. The mixture is formed into small balls or patties and then deep-fried until crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. This savory snack or street food is enjoyed across the Middle East and beyond, known for its delicious flavor and satisfying texture. Ta'meya is often served in a variety of ways, such as stuffed into pita bread with tahini sauce, salad, and pickles, or served as part of a mezze spread alongside other dishes like hummus, tabbouleh, and baba ganoush. With its crispy exterior and soft, flavorful interior, ta'meya is a beloved dish that reflects the rich culinary traditions of the region. Whether enjoyed as a quick bite on the go or as part of a leisurely meal with friends and family, it remains a favorite among food enthusiasts worldwide.
